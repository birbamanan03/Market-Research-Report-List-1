<p><p>The Trimethylolpropane (TMP) flakes market can be categorized into two main production methods: the Sodium Formate Method and the Aldehyde Hydrogenation Method. The Sodium Formate Method involves the reaction of formaldehyde and propylene oxide in the presence of sodium formate, yielding TMP efficiently. In contrast, the Aldehyde Hydrogenation Method uses hydrogenation of acetaldehyde with propylene oxide to produce TMP. Both methods cater to different market needs, influencing the quality, cost, and environmental impact of TMP flakes in industrial applications.</p></p>
