<p><p>The on-board electric vehicle (EV) charger market is primarily divided into two types: single-phase and three-phase chargers. Single-phase chargers typically use a single alternating current (AC) phase, offering a standard charging solution suitable for residential use, with lower power output. In contrast, three-phase chargers employ three AC phases, providing higher power levels, enabling faster charging times, and making them ideal for commercial installations or high-capacity charging stations. Both types cater to different charging needs and infrastructure capabilities within the EV market.</p></p>
