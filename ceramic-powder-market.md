<p><p>The ceramic powder market is categorized into several types based on their chemical compositions and properties. Boride ceramic powders are known for their hardness and high-temperature stability, making them useful in aerospace and defense applications. Carbide ceramic powders offer exceptional wear resistance and thermal conductivity, often used in cutting tools. Nitride ceramic powders exhibit outstanding thermal stability and low thermal expansion, suitable for electronics and high-temperature environments. Oxide ceramic powders are prized for their electrical insulation and chemical stability, while other types encompass specialized ceramic materials for niche applications.</p></p>
