<p><p>Electrochlorination systems are employed to generate chlorine for water treatment, and they primarily come in two types: Brine systems and Seawater systems. Brine systems utilize a concentrated salt solution to produce chlorine, making them ideal for applications with controlled environments and specific salinity levels. In contrast, Seawater systems draw directly from ocean water, utilizing its natural salinity to generate chlorine. Both systems aim to ensure effective disinfection and help maintain water quality in various industrial and municipal applications.</p></p>
