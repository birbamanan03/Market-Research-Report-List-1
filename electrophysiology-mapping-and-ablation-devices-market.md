<p><p>Electrophysiology mapping and ablation devices encompass various technologies used to diagnose and treat cardiac arrhythmias. Conventional devices utilize direct electrode contact for mapping and ablation. Advanced devices incorporate 3D mapping and navigation systems for precise intervention. Ultrasound ablation employs sound waves to create lesions, while cryoablation uses extreme cold to disrupt cardiac tissue. Microwave ablation uses microwave energy for targeted tissue destruction. Each type offers unique advantages in terms of accuracy, effectiveness, and patient outcomes in electrophysiology procedures.</p></p>
