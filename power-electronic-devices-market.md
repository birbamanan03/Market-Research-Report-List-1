<p><p>Power electronic devices are categorized into various types based on their control capabilities. Half-controlled devices, such as thyristors, allow partial control of current flow, enabling efficient regulation in specific applications. Fully-controlled devices, like MOSFETs and IGBTs, provide complete control over current, making them ideal for versatile usage in power electronics. Uncontrolled devices, such as diodes, permit current flow in one direction only, lacking active control. Other market types may include specialized devices that cater to niche applications in power conversion and management.</p></p>
