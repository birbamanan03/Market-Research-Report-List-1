<p><p>Hemostasis and tissue sealing agents are crucial in surgical procedures to control bleeding and promote tissue adhesion. Hemostasis agents are designed to accelerate clot formation and stop bleeding; these include absorbable collagen sponges and thrombin-based products. Tissue sealing agents, on the other hand, facilitate the bonding of tissues, enhancing wound closure and reducing leakage, commonly comprising fibrin sealants and adhesive gels. Together, they improve patient outcomes by minimizing surgical complications and promoting faster healing.</p></p>
