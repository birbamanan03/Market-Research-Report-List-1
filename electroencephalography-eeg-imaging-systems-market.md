<p><p>Electroencephalography (EEG) imaging systems encompass various types, primarily categorized as Conventional EEG, Video EEG, and Dynamic EEG. Conventional EEG captures brain electrical activity through surface electrodes, mainly for routine diagnostics. Video EEG combines brainwave monitoring with synchronized video recording, aiding in the assessment of seizure disorders. Dynamic EEG involves continuous monitoring over extended periods, often in outpatient settings, to capture transient neurological events. Each type serves specific clinical needs, enhancing the understanding and management of neurological conditions.</p></p>
